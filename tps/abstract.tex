Title: Physics-Infused Reduced-Order Modeling for Analysis of Multi-Layered Hypersonic Thermal Protection Systems

Abstract: Despite continued advances in simulation for scientific and engineering applications, many-query aerospace workflows -- such as multi-disciplinary design, analysis, optimization, and control (MDAOC) -- remain limited by a persistent trilemma: simultaneously achieving accuracy, efficiency, and generalizability. For example, classical discretization-based solvers can deliver high-fidelity predictions, yet their online cost scales poorly with increasing state dimension, parametric variability, and temporal resolution, rendering repeated evaluations across MDAOC pipelines computationally intractable. To address this bottleneck, physics-infused reduced-order modeling (PIROM) has emerged as a promising scientific machine learning framework for simulation. By leveraging the Mori-Zwanzig (MZ) coarse-graining formalism, PIROM provides a principled approach for combining physics and data at a reduced-order level, yielding a two-component structure: (i) a physics-based term that encodes low-order dynamics and ensures efficiency and generalizability, and (ii) a data-driven memory term that encodes higher-order physics-data interactions and ensures accuracy. In this work, the PIROM is demonstrated on the simulation of hypersonic thermal protection systems, where the proposed framework consistently outperforms state-of-the-art baselines and provides quantifiable contributions towards the resolution of the trilemma.
