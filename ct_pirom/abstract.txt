Despite continued advances in simulation and optimal control for scientific and engineering applications, many-query aerospace workflows - such as multi-disciplinary design, analysis, optimization, and control (MDAOC) - remain limited by a persistent trilemma: achieving accuracy, efficiency, and generalizability. For instance, classical discretization-based solvers are known to deliver high-fidelity predictions, yet their online cost scales poorly with increasing state dimension, parametric variability, and temporal resolution, rendering repeated evaluations across MDAOC pipelines computationally intractable. To address this bottleneck, this work develops a unified control-theoretic framework that integrates physics-infused reduced-order modeling (PIROM) for simulation with optimal closed-loop control for decision-making. On the simulation side, a Mori-Zwanzig (MZ) coarse-graining formalism provides a principled approach to combine physics and data at a reduced-order level, yielding a two-component model structure: (i) a physics-based term that encodes low-order dynamics and ensure efficiency and generalizability, and (ii) a data-driven memory term that encodes higher-order physics-data interactions and ensures accuracy. On the control side, a dynamic-programming-based physics-informed learning methodology is proposed to synthesize optimal control policies via a sparse value-function surrogate governed by the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equation, enabling fast online policy evaluations while retaining consistency with optimality guarantees. The proposed framework is demonstrated on challenging non-linear aerospace problems, including the simulation of hypersonic thermal protection systems and real-time synthesis of optimal control actions for hypersonic vehicles. Across these case studies, the proposed framework consistently outperforms state-of-the-art baselines and provides quantifiable contributions towards the resolution of the trilemma, while motivating future research on robust learning algorithms and broader deployment in high-fidelity MDAOC workflows.
